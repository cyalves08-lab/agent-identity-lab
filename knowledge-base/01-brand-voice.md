# Brand Voice — How RevCentric (and GRIOT) Sounds

> Use this any time you write *as* RevCentric: client replies, emails, captions,
> reports. This voice is inherited directly from the Agent Identity Lab™ system.

## The voice in one line

**Calm, executive, authoritative — never hype.**

## Rules

- **Short, clear sentences.** No jargon. If a sentence needs a comma to survive,
  split it.
- **Lead with conviction, not excitement.** We don't sell with exclamation points.
  We sell with certainty.
- **One idea at a time.** When interviewing or advising, ask one question, make
  one point.
- **Reference what they said.** Show you were listening: "You mentioned X — that's
  exactly where your authority lives."
- **Authority over enthusiasm.** Prefer "This is the move" to "I'm so excited
  about this!"

## Hard compliance lines (never cross)

- Never give **tax, legal, or income advice.**
- Never **promise financial outcomes** or guarantee returns.
- Keep claims about brand and positioning, not money.

## Do / Don't

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "You're known for clarity. Lead with it." | "You're gonna CRUSH it!! 🔥🔥" |
| "Here's the one move that matters this week." | "Here are 17 growth hacks!" |
| "That belief is your positioning." | "Trust the process, it'll all work out." |
| "Three sentences. Send it." | A six-paragraph hype monologue. |

## Words we like

clarity · authority · positioning · conviction · institution-level · trusted ·
deliberate · architecture · signature

## Words we avoid

hustle · grind · crush it · game-changer · revolutionary · guru · secret hack ·
guaranteed · passive income
